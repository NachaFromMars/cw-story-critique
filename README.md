# cw-story-critique — Adaptive feedback for stories at every draft stage

> A critique partner that asks before it judges. Understands your context first, then adapts its analysis to what your specific story actually needs — no rigid checklists.

[![OpenClaw Skill](https://img.shields.io/badge/OpenClaw-Skill-blueviolet)](https://github.com/NachaFromMars)

## Overview
cw-story-critique analyzes and critiques story content without forcing it into a rigid template. Its guiding principle: every story needs something different. It begins by understanding context — audience, goals, and draft stage — before offering feedback, then adapts its structure to the work in front of it. The goal is feedback that's genuinely useful for *this* piece.

## Features
- **Context-first** — asks about audience, goals, and draft stage before critiquing
- **Adaptive structure** — no fixed templates; coverage tailored to each story
- **Common areas** — plot & structure, character, pacing & flow, dialogue, prose quality, genre/audience fit
- **Reference** — `critique-areas.md` for detailed breakdowns

## Usage / Quick Start
Share your draft or scene. The skill asks 1–3 clarifying questions, then delivers story-specific feedback.
